---
title: "The Orange Tree"
handle: "orange"
---

Long ago, I had a dream.  
It began with a smile that I recognized  
from San Diego, San Mateo, and suburban Texas; a smile  
that wells up from your throat and screams its joy  
so loud it drives the crowd to tears. A dress  
and my sharpest outfit, a white Uniqlo shirt and my silly charcoal suit,  
that I put on far too late in a messy bedroom  
I would clean after the ceremony,  
which I returned to late as always. A crowd  
of friends and family, the people I never managed  
to fully trust, awaiting a first glance  
at the person who would take me away.  

Long ago, my family had an orange tree.  
Mom, Dad, Laryn, Patrick, you remember  
that it never grew any fruit. It sat there, a joke  
whenever we thought of it, or whenever Molly decided to  
hide behind it. And we forgot about it, until a day  
when I was about thirteen when we looked out  
the window and saw a single orange—  
yellow at the time, it had yet to ripen—  
hanging at its center. And on a day  
just a year later, a baker’s dozen hung beneath its leaves.  
I thought I knew we would never have a bloom.  
I wonder if you did, too.  
I never asked.
